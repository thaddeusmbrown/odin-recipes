Picadillo

Picadillo is a savory Cuban dish that is easy to prepare. A balanced mixture of vegetables, meat, starch, and spices, it is sure to bring joy to the dinner table. Credit to lutzflcat at allrecipes.com for the recipe.

Ingredients

  • 2 potatoes
  • salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion minced
  • 1 green bell pepper, minced
  • 2 teaspoons crushed garlic
  • 1-1/2 pounds lean ground beef
  • 2 cups dry red wine
  • 1 (15 ounce) can tomato sauce
  • 2/3 cup pimiento-stuffed green olives, drained and chopped
  • 1/2 cup capers, drained
  • 1/4 cup golden raisins
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 pinch dried oregano
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon olive oil

Steps

  1. Place potatoes in a large pot covered in water. Season with salt and bring hte water to a boil. Reduce heat to medium-low and cook the potatoes covered at a simmer until tender (about 20 minutes). Drain and set aside to cool.
  2. Heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a large Dutch oven over medium heat. Cook and stir the onion, green bell pepper, and garlic until the vegetables soften (about 5 minutes).
  3. Crumble the ground beef in the vegetable mixture, crumbling as you cook. Brown beef completely (7 to 10 minutes) and drain as much fat as possible.
  4. Stir the red wine, tomato sauce, olives, capers ,golden raisins, cumin, oregano, black pepper, and 1/4 teaspoon salt into the ground beff mixture. Bring to a boil, reduce heat to medium-low, and simmer until the wine has reduced (25 to 30 minutes).
  5. Peel the cooled potatoes and cut into chunks.
  6. Heat 1 teaspoon olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Cook the potatoes in the hot oil until browned (about 5 minutes).
  7. Gently fold the cooked potatoes into the ground beef mixture and cook together for about 5 minutes.
